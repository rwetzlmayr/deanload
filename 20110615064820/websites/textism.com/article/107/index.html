Descriptive Services:: 6 APRIL 01

I’ve never understood them, but I keep coming back to Whit Stillman movies. I suppose it’s the dialogue, the hyperbanal preppie earnestness, the declarative minutiae:

“Do you think a hare would ever lose a race to a tortoise? Not on your life.”
“I prefer the term UHBs: Urban Haute Bourgeoisie”
“Anything I did that was wrong, I apologize for. Anything that was not wrong, I don’t apologize for.”

As I write this, The Last Days of Disco is on television. For whatever reason the audio track for the visually impaired is broadcasting as well, so whenever there’s a break in the dialogue, this inane, perky female voice kicks in:

“Josh begins to dance seductively toward Alice.”
“Dan and Holly continue necking on the couch.”
“Alice looks in weary chagrin toward the closed door.”

It’s like Whit Stillman times ten.
