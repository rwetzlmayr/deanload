Punditry · 28 February 01

For some reason I’m going to be on Robert Mason Lee’s On the Edge next week, a television program based around people trying to speak in complete sentences, but who often find themselves unable. Half an hour of intense Canadian cultural insight, broadcast to a nation gasping for same. Actually, it can be quite good, notwithstanding Mason Lee’s ambitious shirt selections. It’s usually tighter than the oddly similarly-named spinoff on CBC, in which Steve Burgess needs to slow the hell down and stop reusing ideas from NPR.

The theme of the episode (“the Web”) seems a little narrow and constrictive at first glance, but I’m sure something will come of it. Please don’t watch.
