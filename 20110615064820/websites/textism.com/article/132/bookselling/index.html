Bookselling · 21 April 01

Columnar fuckwit Paul Brent of the National Socialist phones one in about the celebrity backslapping going on at Ottawa’s Competition Bureau over the ashes of the Canadian bookselling industry. Thundering dunderhead Paul Brent thinks this pretend “regulating” should evaporate, to allow “real competition to flourish by relaxing the restrictions on foreign booksellers.” Moron.

And while things are looking dire in the Canadian book trade, with appeals blocked and no possibility of claims for the next three years, American independent booksellers have been lawyered into submission and litigated up the ass, legitimate antitrust concerns snuffed, and so I guess bye bye to them too. Here’s to Howard Johnson homogeneity and do-or-die profit motives and literature as brand signifier and writing that fits into nice little niches and blank books and pens and gosh I think we have that but I couldn’t possibly tell you where.
