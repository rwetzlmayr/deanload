Ahem · 25 April 2001

Forthcoming in July is a strange and wonderful week, during which graphic design technique, theory and opinion will cloud the air. The Summer Design Intensive – part of Simon Fraser University’s renowned Summer Publishing Workshops – is an exhaustive, week-long hands-on insanely fun frenzy of visual thinking, directed by some guy named Dean Allen.

Lab work on the latest digital fetish objects is interspersed with presentations on matters such as typography, art direction, web design, information design, identity and packaging, and on new technologies like OpenType and InDesign. Participants work on a specific project while interacting with designers of varied backgrounds and expertise. At the end of the week, gurus come in to critique the work. And then we all get very drunk.

People come in from all over the world for this workshop; those who’ve been putting off a summer visit to Vancouver may find all excuses now exhausted.
