Professional Development · 1 March 01

‘Bullshit’ certainly appeared in my own conversation that day when I flipped through my first book of management theory – Tom Peters’ 800-page 1992 opus, Liberation Management. Peters’ zanier 1997 book, The Circle of Innovation, is bullshit on wheels.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;—Tom Frank, One Market Under God.

Thank heaven for the gurus – masters of business boosterism whose fantasies of a better way come tumbling down in books, seminars, journals with soothing bolshevism, with hardcore obsequiousness. They are admirable. They are energetic. They work up a sweat at the edges of convention, then smash on through with the earnest fecklessness required to come up with a term like brandwidth.

Since I happened upon this nugget a few weeks ago I’ve been trying to write something about it, but I’m giving up. Satire is drained of any purpose on the same planet as Tom Peters’ PowerPoint slides.
