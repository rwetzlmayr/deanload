May Day II · 1 May 2001

I think I’ll haul this out every year. It’s such nutty fun:

“The A-16 activists and their many sympathizers say that a new political movement was born in the tumult and tear gas of Seattle. It’s beginning to look as if they are at least half-right. Something different is certainly taking form. But is it really so new? On the contrary: In its mistrust of technology, its antagonism for rational thought, its veneration of tribal folkways, its disdain for electoral politics and its celebration of violence, the antiglobalization cause is looking increasingly familiar – it is looking more and more like the ultranationalist mass movements that swept Europe in the 1920s and 1930s. They don’t yet have the charismatic leader and the snappy salute. But give them time, give them time.”

— David Frum, National Post, Apr 15, 2000
