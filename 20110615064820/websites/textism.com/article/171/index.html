Twenty Faces :: 7 MAY 01

For your consumption: Twenty Faces, an examination of some of the better type families designed for extended reading, with highly subjective notes on usage and what to expect. (Eventually it will be thirty and, I hope, a lot less jury-rigged).

When I’m teaching – pacing, sub-referencing, falling into tangents, speaking too fast – I talk a great deal about evaluating type based on the requirements of the text: a complex process based on thousands of conditions, informed by years of study and experience. Inevitably someone asks, “can’t you just tell us which ones to use?”

I’d rather not of course, but hey, school’s out for summer.
