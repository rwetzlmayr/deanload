Great Leap Forward · 18 May 2001

Those interested in graphic design and propaganda (is there a difference?) will lose several hours at this remarkable Czech site boasting an amazing collection of Soviet, East European and Cuban posters.

It’s striking how much more gentle, lyrical and elegiac the Czech posters are compared to the Russian ones, notwithstanding the common theme; also notable is the recurring faintly homoerotic imagery of the Soviet posters.

What’s most beguiling is the prevalence of hand-drawn display type (long past the time when machine-generated type dominated in the West). And do keep an eye out for the one-sheet triptychs: Lenin keeps being broken up, gesturing and pontificating, across three huge posters. And the composition! The composition!
