Nine Short Essays · 19 May 01

A timely and relevant piece from 1996: Nine Short Essays by Jerrold Maddox, whose sane contemplation of the role of design on the web – and on the practice and pleasures of reading online and off – might well have been written yesterday. On the matter of (then nascent) wellsprings of multimediocrity such as DHTML, Java and Shockwave:

[T]hese disturbing and distracting ways of approaching Web design ask to be addressed with some searching questions and thoughtful work. We need to learn about how to best make it reveal beautiful things and not make the container the object of attention.

Word. And a gem from the poet Elizabeth Bishop:

I admire compression, lightness, and agility,
all rare in this loose world.
