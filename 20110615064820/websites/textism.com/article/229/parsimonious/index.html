Parsimonious · 6 June 01

When I decided to go work for myself back in late ’97, after years as a book designer and art director, my ambition was to start publishing books that, in form at least, responded to two problems dogging current trade publishing: bad market-obsessed design and ridiculous pricing.

The idea was to make small, affordable, conservatively but elegantly designed books in the chapbook tradition; books entirely without a lifestyle component, explicitly made for those who like to read, and available to everyone (compared, say, to the latest $35 novel which will be affordable in paper once its hardcover market is tapped). Did this plan ever happen? Of course not – this is me we’re talking about: lord and master of unfinished ideas.

Nonetheless, what a pleasure to find out about a publisher who’s pursuing almost exactly the same idea. The books of Parsimony Press could use a little typographic massage, yet are already so far above the nearly nonexistent design standards at most trade houses it’s hardly worth quibbling.

Brilliant, brilliant, brilliant.
