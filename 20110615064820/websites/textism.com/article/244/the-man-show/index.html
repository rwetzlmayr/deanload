The Man Show · 13 June 2001

Nothing quite so unsettling as catching oneself in predictable behaviour.

I’m getting groceries a couple weeks ago, right, and in the juice aisle there’s a bottle of noxious-looking green liquid, a thick mix of organic fruit and wheatgrass, spirulina, kelp, whatever. There may have been hay. How the label did brag! Free-radical eliminators, rare nutrients, vitamins, essential fatty Arbuckles. I think, gosh, you know, I’ve been feeling a little run down lately, surely this will be just the thing to get the pep back, and I toss it in the basket.

First thing in the morning, for a few days, I’d pull the bottle from the fridge and take a chewy swig off it, making a bleaugh sound and an unpleasant face once I’d forced it down. Health, health, health, I’d mutter, gathering coffee and cigarettes.

Blink. Two weeks pass. I’m rooting around for the bright yellow mustard when I see the forgotten bottle of health in the back of the fridge has taken on new form: it’s bigger. Sort of bloated. The straight sides of semi-opaque plastic have ballooned out, indicating fermentation, or at least a great deal of pressure. So, naturally enough, I pick up the bottle and open the cap.

BAM! The lid ricochets off the cupboard, DING! hits the light fixture. A few seconds later I’m standing there, blinking, slightly shaken and deeply impressed, as this spooky fog of biomatter wafts out of the bottle.

But before long I start thinking about the bright yellow mustard again, and what do I do? I take the cap, conveniently come to rest on the counter in front of me, put it back on the bottle, and return the bottle to the fridge.
