Creative Man  ::   JUN 18

Philip Marchand, in his excellent book Ripostes, does a cross-referenced analysis of Margaret Atwood’s novels, identifying in each the author’s surrogate character (stoic, innocent, transformed). More interestingly, he nails Atwood’s habit of pairing her fictional surrogates with sensitive but slightly ridiculous men. The partners usually pursue some creative career – composing difficult music, building kinetic sculpture – with zero likelihood of success.

It’s pretty easy to laugh at limp artistic ambition. So let’s: enjoy the work of Tony Curtis, David Bowie, Robert Goulet, Richard Chamberlain, George Clinton and Micky Dolenz.

UPDATE – Nearly forgot Buddy Ebsen.
