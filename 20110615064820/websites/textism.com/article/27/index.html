ClearHype :: 5 MARCH 01

A retyped press release in the Globe and Mail today promises that Microsoft will lead us out of the abject shittiness of onscreen reading, maybe, someday. No really. Another dance of hype about new type technologies meant to pique investor interest and do nothing to fix the damage already done. GX, Unicode, TrueType, OpenType: you’ll recall how central to the working experience these technologies were. Now ClearType, announced years ago, is up to bat.

Soon, hold steady now, there will be proprietary MS hardware (a tablet) using proprietary MS font-rendering technology, and the millions of users who will continue using illegible, downright hostile reading interfaces like Word will apparently have to go quietly away.

Still, the article offers some astonishing new insight into the MS lifestyle:

It’s a laid-back place considering the high-pressure work, and this West Coast attitude is reflected in the absence of dress code (Birkenstocks and shorts meet dress shoes and trousers in the hallways) and presence of toys such as dart boards, pinball machines, and putters scattered about common spaces.

WHAT ARE YOU SAYING, MAN?
