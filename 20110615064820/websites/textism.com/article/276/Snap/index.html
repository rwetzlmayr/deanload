Snap · 29 June 2001

I have this idea for a business book, one of those pseudo-Talmudic middle-management thingmabobs that come out every few minutes, focused on a proven business model. I call it The Way of the Reckless Chiropractor.

Roll with me here: you develop a relationship with a client desperate for help: in pain – actual, tangible agony – and you provide relief. But the relief you’re singularly able to provide will only work if you come back on Thursday. And, well, if you really want to be well, to be free of pain (can you see the metaphor just steaming here?), you’ll have to come back to be smacked and snapped and twisted and thumped three times a week for the rest of your life. Isn’t your wellbeing, and the wellbeing of your shareholders, worth it?

I swear, the people who buy this crap would lap it up like canned peaches.

Acquisition editors may present offers at the usual addresses.
