Teardown :: 17 JULY 01

Something I’m learning from the disassembly of my domestic surround in advance of the move: it appears that, for the past four years, I’ve been incapable of throwing anything away. Anything. In these four rooms there are, among other things:

  • Wodges of ATM receipts, pulled out and stuffed somewhere once my wallet felt too thick.
  • A small pile of yellow and black paper tags, ripped from the lowest buttonhole of laundered shirts and flicked (hockey-card style, overhand) into the space between the top of the dresser and the small drawer that was probably designed to hold something other than loose change, lint and an old clarinet reed.
  • Seven kinds of mustard.
  • Jurassic technology unused for ages: a Dynaco tube amp; a Palm Pro; a Linotype Saphir scanner (SCSI) I think I’m still paying for; a handheld GPS positioning device acquired while briefly held by the idea of an end to wilderness; four Mac Pluses.
  • Stock photo catalogues from agencies long since assimilated.
  • A toaster (luxury model: four slices, bagels, whatever), inoperable for years (my friend Steve used to fantasize about a toaster so powerful it would dim the lights; this was that toaster, for awhile).
  • Every magazine, book and newspaper I’ve purchased.
  • Boxes hastily crammed with stuff on moving day, four or five moves ago.
  • Trousers, suits, hats that will never be worn again.
