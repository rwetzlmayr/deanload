Lecture Preparation · 21 July 01

THE ALLEN APPROACH

In the weeks beforehand
Arrive at vague, loosely connected concepts whilst showering or washing the dishes. Scribble occasional notes on cigarette packs or whatever is lying around (you will never see these notes again).

In the days beforehand
Click around Google, late at night, gathering images to punctuate your point. Worry that you’re utterly unprepared. Overcome this worry by going out to drink coffee and scowl at passersby with your head in a book that’s sort of vaguely connected to the subject of the lecture.

In the hours beforehand
Decide that your presentation is crap. Time for a complete rewrite. No time to shower or shave! Learn new presentation software (you need the edge) in thirty minutes. Realize that gathered images, notes and ideas are three ships (without captains) on collision course. Work the various bits into an enormous document of non sequiturs and uncompleted ideas. Just as it starts to make sense, your computer will crash. During restart, scream faaaaaaaaawwck into your fist.

In the minutes beforehand
Mary Mother of God the presentation actually works; the visuals are in place, there is cohesion, wit, there is a nice arc there toward the end. Distill to Acrobat and luxuriate in the seven minutes you have to clean up and get dressed while you burn to CD. At some point as you towel, button, lace, zip, you hear the ping of Toast Death: of a failed burn. The next twenty minutes, if filmed, would make fine slapstick comedy of the early silent era. Also, you realize you haven’t eaten anything all day.

Take the stairs two at a time, arrive breathless (oh! how you swore you’d arrive early this time) realizing you have no idea what you’re going to say.

Yet it always seems to go well.
