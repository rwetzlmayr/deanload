Boom · 8 August 01

Some vaguely pleasing things about war and men:

Robert McNamara, the stooge American potentate of the police operation also known as the Vietnam War, maybe the only person in the whole 25-year debacle who, with a sense of duty and honour, simply followed orders; and who, in the last ten years, has stated, firmly, in an unwavering voice, “We Were Wrong” to anyone who would bother to listen; nowadays enjoys quiet evenings at home, relaxing with friends, taking and retaking the SATs.

Terry Glavin – the best writer presently living and working in this idyllic lotusland that I am about to depart – once summarized the Balkan conflict as people killing each other over differences in folk dancing.

My great-uncle Art, who died a few years ago, entered the RCAF with the express intention of becoming a Tailgunner, a member of what was called the 20-minute club, a term derived from the statistical life expectancy of those holding the position. As it happens he was only able to train whilst divebombing whales off the coast of Vancouver Island before World War II ended.

Lewis McKenzie, the Canadian NATO thingy during the first sweep through Bosnia, has an affection for go-kart racing.

Romeo Leblanc, the Canadian NATO thingy during the insanity in Rwanda, still hasn’t gotten over it.
