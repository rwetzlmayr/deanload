Transit :: 13 AUGUST 01

Just now, at 2:59 in the morning, I fell out of the chaos of my semi-empty home and walked 200 metres to the 7-11 on the corner, whereupon I acquired fast food and beverage, padding back through the alley which is never not occupied by the tireless labourers of dumpster mining, and just now, at 2:42 in the morning, there were no less than three.

We nod and smile.

Two years ago I was walking back from breakfast, gazing at my toes, not thinking at all about this alley and its traffic – about its highly qualified refuseniks – when (dah!) I found a $20 bill just lying there. Just resting there.

I’m moving to a 300 year-old stone house in the suburbs of Pompignan (pop. 600) in the Languedoc, near Pont-du-Gard and Ganges, near Montpellier, near Nîmes, in the South of France; to spend languid days and nights with a beautiful, ludicrously smart woman, with whom I am deeply, irrevocably in love.

Still a little unclear on this happiness business.
