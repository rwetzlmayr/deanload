Regulate :: 7 SEPTEMBER 01

“Our Germans are better than their Germans.”
—The Right Stuff

Replace ‘Germans’ with ‘lawyers’ and there’s your dominant paradigm, sonny Jim.

Safe to say it no longer matters who has god and guns and furious anger on their side. Because of lawyers, Dubya is leader of the free world, Microsoft runs rampant and Tom Wolfe negotiates massive advances for smallminded, shitty novels unburdened by observation or convincing dialogue.

So say you don’t like the way things are decided on your behalf. You resist notions of an unimpeachably perfect free market, or that business will always – ho ho – regulate itself, or that places outside North America and Western Europe are a plundering ground of free labour. Maybe it seems a tad contradictory to blow billions of dollars up the asses of bankers and insurers while demonizing schoolteachers and squeegee kids.

What do you do? Taking to the streets, it must be said, has zero impact on policy, and indeed it does little more than help simpering idiots fill column inches. At dinner the other night herself suggested that simply living a decent life, not partaking of the spoils of madmen, and helping out when possible was the best compromise. My own lifelong dedication to mockery and denunciation hasn’t yet given a moment’s pause to Alan Greenspan or Tony “How far would you like me to bend over” Blair.

I do, however, have an idea.

If policy only ever changes at the hands of lawyers, and lawyers only ever go where the money is, force the little weasels into community service immediately after passing the bar, in advance of a career of crimes against humanity. Just as doctors must complete hospital residency before the subscription to Pacific Yachting kicks in, conscript lawyers to spend a couple years working for non-profits, media watchers, observers of business practice, human rights organizations, publishers, arts and science advocacies, all those who don’t see capital as both means and end.

And then, after the allotted time, they can go fuck up the world. Merely a thought.
