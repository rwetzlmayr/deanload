Radio On · 9 March 01

The redoubtable Paul Tough, biding time in the indefinite pause of Open Letters – a truly brilliant online accomplishment, despite the occasional blotch – before he undertakes a situation at a certain newspaper of record, is spending the month guest starring on a new radio project called Transom, which looks rather cool.

Paul has been a contributing editor on This American Life, the 30% twee wank, 70% genius NPR radio show, which I frequently like, especially when Sarah Vowell is on. Look up David Sedaris’ star turns, Jack Hitt’s fiasco, the episode of “Canadians Among Us,” hell, listen to the whole fucking archive. Ira grows on you.

Transom, the new project, looks like an interesting marriage of This American Life’s personal narratives and rich documentary with some new online tweaks, and is open to all.

Odds are even it’ll be better than Out Front, even with Neal Pollack attached.
