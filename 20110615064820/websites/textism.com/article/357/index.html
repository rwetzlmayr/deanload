Think Tank - 13 September 01

I read Ann Coulter’s shrill, hilarious scream for attention amid all this death and sadness, though there’s no need for you to bother (if you do go there, carry with you the image of brittle, horrible Ann, caked in crackling pancake and ghoulish mascara, a run in her beige Sears pantyhose, stamping her feet aghast at a five minute airport delay).

And then, rather as one keeps tonguing a sore tooth to see if it still hurts, I took a boo at how the dingbats at the National Post were fellating their seven paying subscribers in this time of sorrow.

Dead white male Mark Steyn blames school spelling bees for bleeding defence dry, then bends over to take it up the ass from his lord and master, the American Taxpayer.

Former welfare recipient Diane Francis concludes that the attack was not one against the US or NATO, rather it was an attack on gentle, loving CEOs, and on the selfless and giving advocates of global trade deregulation.

And on a street corner in suburban Washington DC, David Frum is accosting passersby, pleading for someone, anyone, to listen.
