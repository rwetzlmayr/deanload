Loyal · 22 March 2002

You know how the account planners and ad hacks have been bouncing around these ideas of brand ecstasy: they write long essays about the anthropology of consumer desire, about the structured intimacy of clarifying one’s own image and choosing a life path while picking up a few things at the supermarket. Well of course it’s galling. It’s irritating that any twerp anywhere can quantify/qualify your own desire when it’s so freaking hard just to make up your mind when presented with a display of goddamned chocolate bars at the 7–11. Not that that’s been an issue lately, but still.

Even more galling, duh, is when it works. Amidst the below-mentioned gardening frenzy I’ve found myself falling head over heels for a brand, and a crashingly unsexy one at that. Gardena makes form-follows-function gardening tools, all designed quite smartly, built to last, affordable. Aisles at the bricolage stores teem with teal and grey and orange gadgets that all interlock and look really neat and just make sense to the upstart peckerwood green thumb like me. I sometimes pause while my brain froths away over some junk (no matter the context, not even in the garden) and I think, you know, this would be much easier with a snappy new tool made by Gardena. And my eyes cast upward, and I smile.

Or it could just be that I like a brand with my name in it.
