Translucent Shopping Bags · 18 March 2001

I’m reading Evelyn Lau’s latest chunk of autobiography, Inside Out, girding up for the inevitable gruesome details of bonking W.P. Kinsella.

The package: one of those twee little hardcovers that women with ironically nerdy glasses buy to augment the Real Simple décor of their lives. The design apes Chip Kidd’s two-layered faux-vellum-over-PLC cardigan for Disclosure by Michael Crichton. Here the vellum carries half the title (Inside) and the inner cover carries the other half (Out). Get it?

Meh.

I always take the cardigan off when reading hardcovers, and thus I’m now reading a book called Out.

Far more appropriate to Lau’s writing – which, I must say, rings clear as a bell notwithstanding the subject matter – was the cover for her collection Fresh Girls, as far as I know the first Canadian literary effort sent out into the market wearing a beaver shot on the front.

Inside, Goudy Old Style with fake small caps, comb-over leading and Douglas-fir em dashes with spaces on either side. Nothing against Goudy, other than toward its tendency to make everything read like it was written by Lewis Lapham. But alongside Bembo display type, it’s kind of like scotch on breakfast cereal. Not the ideal environment in which to read passages like:

I would wipe off the vomit that had spattered my arms and feel as energized as someone who had just woken from a nap or returned from an invigorating jog around the block.
