Purple Air · 26 March 01

Whosoever has purchased a takeaway carton of lobster bisque from the soup place down the street and brought it into his home, and, despite it being rather good with crusty bread and a tumbler of plonk, not quite finished the carton, whosoever has absentmindedly placed the unfinished carton of lobster bisque, its lid closed, in an unobvious place, a place where it might happen to sit forgotten for a couple weeks, perhaps even three, whosoever has come upon such a carton one Sunday evening and wondered, “hey, what’s this,” and without pause removed its lid, well, let it be said he would do well to pause.

One is reminded of the scene in Raiders of the Lost Ark: the porcine Nazi looking at that which he should not have looked, his face being melted, et cetera.
