Creative Destruction:: 30 MARCH 01

In keeping with the notion that everything ever imagined can somehow be turned into evidence of the unquestionable perfection of the market, two new books cast a loving revisionist glance back at the nutbar Austrian economist Joseph Schumpeter, whose masochistic ideas of unanaesthetized corporate self-surgery are a great source of comfort when, say, putting 5000 people out of work to please the fund managers.

Meanwhile, the most ludicrous scheme ever to energize a dwindling internet gold rush (complete with manifestos, slogans and Gueveran imagery) isn’t receiving much warmth, or even kindness.
