Paul Ford:

  

As I sat – nay, contemplated thoughtfully; who has time for mere sitting? – and considered the ideal length to which the nascent scrub under my chin, which is masquerading as a “beard,” should be permitted to grow (the suggestion made by one impolite reader of the Vancouver Sun Article that I “put some milk on that so the cat can lick it off” of course summarily discarded as illiterate chimpery) I heard the sound of Herself in the kitchen putting away the dishes.

Sneezing once more, and idly scratching my perpetually swollen and agitated genitals, I expelled all considerations of beardedness from my mind, for it had been merely a trifle, and the vastness of my conscious ability had suddenly seized upon a truly great problem, a problem even greater than my eternal search to find the edges of my ego (for to plumb all infinite depths is the duty of true and brilliant minds). The problem I set myself upon, a problem to occupy me in the few free moments between judging the inferiority of other electronic artists to myself, lamenting the current state of Canadian cultural thought (implying, and I admit the obvious paradox, that Canada has a unique culture), posting images of my Oliver, and whining indolence, was this: to find the exact typeface which corresponded most closely to the form of Herself.

In my mind I keep a library of over 90,000 typefaces, whether inscribed in stone, wood, or bytes. Show me the merest shard of a descender, and I will cry out “Jansen,” “New Century Schoolbook”, or God forbid, “Hobo.” Yet while the catalog is vast, I narrowed it quickly to a few choice specimens. At first I considered she might be most like Sabon – classic grace and elegance, yet modernized through Tschichold’s intelligent sense of abstraction – an intelligence not unlike my own. But her face, I reasoned (and how did I reason, reader! I reasoned so that Carlyle, Kant, and Wittgenstein rose from their graves and faced France.) has the elegance of the lowercase “g” of Perpetua. And yet still again her legs are pure sans-serif, as fine as the “h” of Frutiger – the same Frutiger designed, of course, for the signage of a Paris airport, and thus emininently fitting for her current situation.

Of such great force were my mental processes, churning through typefaces and matching them to her features, that, momentarily, I froze in a total confusion borne of the massive intellectual pressures that occur across the span of my cerebellum. She emerged from the kitchen, having put away the dishes, and her smile gave thaw to my sudden block – I came out of my state of total concentration with a solution. A lesser mind could not have arrived at the conclusion so readily, I admit, but mine is no lesser mind, and I had found my point of realization: that she was unto herself a typeface most divine, and could not be compared. The contrast of Herself with the slope of Garamond’s italic descenders was futile, given her substance in the flesh and blood before me. (Although her nose does have a certain shade reminiscent of the “C” in Caslon.)
