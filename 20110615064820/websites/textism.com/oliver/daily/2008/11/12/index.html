Oliver searches for the black truffle (Tuber Melanosporum), often considered the king of mushrooms. Its season is from autumn to early spring. Truffles usually occur at the interface between the organic litter and the mineral soil, about one to six inches deep.

Truffles are found mainly in Provence (southeast France), Perigord (southwest France) and Burgundy (Bourgogne). The southeast of France produces 70% of French truffles. In a normal year about 50 tonnes of black truffles are obtained.

Finding the black truffle is comparable to a treasure hunt. Dogs have been trained to sniff out the elusive truffles. A good truffle dog is worth his or her weight in black diamonds. That value has translated into truffle-dog rustling in some areas in the South of France.

Be careful, Oliver, don’t let a rustler sneak up on you!
