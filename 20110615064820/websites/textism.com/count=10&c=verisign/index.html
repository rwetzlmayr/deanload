WTF? - 9 April 02

(Updated)

NetSol, who are by any objective measure a stumbling army of stumbling boobs that stumbles more from year to year, have sold the great Leslie Harpold’s hoopla.com domain out from underneath her (it was registed by one Hubert Sarah of Berlin).

How can this be? It used to be three months after a registration slipped past expiry before it could be sold. When did it expire? Who do we deluge with protests?

As Rob Lowe said on The West Wing when the Republicans started manoeuvering around his affair with the prostitute who was also the transvestite on Ally Mcbeal (which remains the very worst television show ever produced): ‘Mr President, something’s happened and it’s got me hot as a pistol!’

UPDATE Arthur Jennings writes:

This sort of thing has been happening a lot lately (PCWorld, Guardian). Network Solutions can’t be counted on to provide a grace period or notify owners of impending domain name expiration (CNN, MeFi).
They even offer a paid service to help those who want to jump on a domain name as soon as it expires.

Keegan writes:

I know (knew) that site. Quite like it, too. If NetSol is too intractable, I say petition Hubert. It could be an exercise in creative politeness . . . you know present him one big beautiful pleading note signed by, oh, a few thousand very nice blog junkies.

Sounds good to me.

UPDATE Leslie writes:

The name wasn’t set to expire until June. They think (no one is willing to commit to any answer) there may have been a faxed request (faked? forged?) to have the domain transferred to this Sarah person which gave them “my” permission for the transfer (naturally I wrote no such letter, sent no such fax, and I haven’t been in Germany since 1995). So the person swiped it, simply by faxing a forged letter to NetSol as best I can tell. I got no notice via email or land mail, they just did it on the authority of a fax.
I’ve made a bunch of calls, written them another fax with my info and have been told to wait up to 48 hours which is about 10 minutes from now. I called a couple times for progress notes in the interim and was given really clearly scripted answers by customer support. No one who answers the phone is willing to connect me to someone who works on this issue which I find deeply disturbing.

This sucks hard. Does anyone have turboing information for NetSol or Aitdomains (who handed out the reregistration)?

Say - 27 June 01

I do think you might acknowledge how calm I am after ordeals undergone and lessons learned in establishing once and for all that free DNS services are free for a reason. To anyone who sent email to cardigan.com in the last day or so: those messages have been lost to the air and will go unanswered (unlike, say, all the other email I don’t answer).

But while free DNS services merely cause grief, Network Solutions sucks all that is good and true from the universe. You’d think that after having its monopoly slapped out of its hands the company might try a tad harder, but, oh, no.

On the positive side: Luke (whose Qube is the new host for cardigan and textism) is an exceptionally excellent admin: there were some MySQL setup issues which were resolved, I learned, once he “went downtown and bought a book.”

Now that’s what I call tech support.
