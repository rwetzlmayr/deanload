Refer, Word Cleaner - 10 July 03

Refer 2.02 2.03 is now available. Fixed a bunch of silliness, added the ability to prevent recording of referrers on specific directories, and patched a potential security vulnerability. It’s also running about ten times faster than before, but as God is my witness, I have no idea why.

This site’s Word HTML Cleaner has been improved as well: it now no longer strips useful attributes from image tags, hyperlinks and tables, and is generally behaving in a more grownup fashion.

One could conclude from the vigorous workout the Word HTML cleaner is getting from Monday to Friday each week (usage drops off on the weekend) that most of those benefitting from it are doing so at work. If you’re one of them, do keep in mind that there’s no shame in having your employer pay for services that make your job easier. Hell, I’ll even send an impeccably typeset receipt.

Also, companies interested in licensing installations of the Word cleaner as part of their editorial flow can contact me for more information.

Textile 2 - 8 July 03

Is I think ready to show its face for testing. After a lot of interesting back-and-forth with Perl slinger capo di tutti capi Brad Choate, what started out earlier this year as a simple syntax for transforming plain text into structurally valid, typographically rich web text is now much more powerful, though I hope just as simple to use.

Additions include the ability to quickly create tables like this:

|a|table|row|
|a|table|row|

...to mix and match list types, to change alignment and indentation of block objects with a single character, and to apply class, id, style and language attributes to block, phrase and table elements. For example, to quickly make a phrase red without a lot of fussing with tags, then %{color:red}this is all that’s required%. To make a paragraph right-justified, it need only begin with p>. Left-indenting a paragraph is as simple as this: p(.

Also new is the option to store fugly link URLs outside of the text you’re working in, so instead of having to navigate text littered with "link":http://incrediblylongurl.com/deep/drilling/, you can reference the long URLs like so: "link":url (thanks to Gruber for suggesting this).

This version is beta, but not too volatile. There’s now a form at the bottom of the Textile result page to send in bug reports.

Those brave enough to wade through the current PHP code will find it here.

Days Like These - 28 June 03

On this day two of the swellest-most excellent good people alive (that being he and she) are getting married, in New York City. That’s pretty sweet, that.

Here in Pompignan, on an unremarkable weekend save for a rooster crowing championship and the monster truck rally Sunday night in St Hippolyte, I have declared this to be «Fête du Carrie et Jeffrey».

At dusk I’ll be ascending the hillside where, with the help of a diesel generator, two Marshall stacks and knockoff Les Paul, I shall fill the valley with a long, feedback-rich solo rendition of Mendelssohn’s “Wedding March”, followed by a few light favourites.

Refer 2.01 - 23 June 03

An update to everyone’s favourite vanity stockticker is available.

Those using Refer 2.0 should replace all existing files (refer.css hasn’t changed, however), fill in the preferences required by refer.php and this is important: run setupdb.php again. This won’t wipe out existing referrer data, but will simply tweak the Refer database table for improved performance, stability, robustness and suchlike hooey.

With this version, among other improvements, Refer offers a ‘glimpse’ of the top incoming site referrers recorded during the preceding 1, 2, 3 and 7 days.
