Caca Nana Dada:: 1 JULY 01

It being Canada Day, I feel a small surge of hoserism coursing through me. And as a soon-to-be expatriate, I feel compelled to point out notable Canuckleheads living – as they say in Newfoundland – away:

In London, there is Craig Taylor, who’s written a review of everything he’s ever encountered; in Chigago, Chester, a huge storm cloud over party shoes; of course you all know Heather and her lethal eyes in San Francisco; and then there’s that most beautiful and beguiling woman living in the South of France.

Canadians remaining as yet North of the 49: Soap Box Girls, whom I like although I can’t hope to understand the sponge thing; Riley Dog, who writes terse; wood s lot, who quotes long, long, long; Clark, who writes circles around his detractors, there’s Toastboy in Montréal, who’s pretty fucking smart; Mike Dewit here in Lotusland, funnier than you are; Grant Splorp in Edmonton, who should post more than once a month; and a hundred others whom I read regularly.

Bid Adieu:: 21 JUNE 01

It may be that you’ve heard enough in recent weeks about the demise of Suck and Feed, but what the hell do I care, here’s my two cents.

I loved both publications, full stop. Even when Suck went on autopilot in ’99, even after Feed introduced the idiotic Monsanto logotype. Feed because it was smart and arch and funny and seemed always to be about things I’m interested in. Suck because its founders were insanely good writers – that is if you believe, as I do, that good writers improve on what they imitate. Carl and Joey shamelessly aped the withering voice of The Baffler (rightly so; it was the most forceful and clever critical writing of the time) while avoiding that magazine’s dour earnestness. And they hired on great people: the ludicrously intelligent Ana Marie Cox (on whom I had a boyish crush for months), the terribly funny Heather Havrilesky and, er, Tim Cavanaugh.

I loved it so much that four years ago I sent in a horribly written, faintly snide nyah-nyah letter, in which I somehow name-dropped Suck, The Baffler, Cox, Infinite Jest, Burning Man and Barbara Gowdy in one go. Within minutes I got a scathing reply from Joey Anuff, in which I recall he ably tore me a new one, adding that he couldn’t care less what “poor little rich boy” Tom Frank and his Baffler minions thought.

Two years later, I was clicking randomly around eBay bidding on old magazines, when I saw that Jim Romenesko was auctioning his copy of the seminal and rare Baffler Number Five, and the current high bidder was one joey@wired.com. I bid, and for two days we inched up in 50¢ increments until the auction closed. I still have it, here, on the bookcase by my desk. I should put it up on eBay.

20 Things:: 20 JUNE 01

Last month I signed on to Judith Calamazoo’s 20 Things, 20 People, 20 Days Give or Take project, in which 20 people from hither and yon make 20 pieces of art, which are sent to Judith, who then sends out packages of 20 different things.

So I bulked up, grew a beard, stood in front of a paint-spattered boombox playing “A White Shade of Pale,” became obsessed with the nape of Rosanna Arquette’s neck (I helped her, loved her, but she left me for Steve Buscemi), and eventually, working through the pain, I produced this.
